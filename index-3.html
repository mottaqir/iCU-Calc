Shock Classification & Differentiator ▼
ALL SHOCK: MAP <65 or SBP <90 + End-organ hypoperfusion
Cool/mottled skin · Oliguria (<0.5 ml/kg/hr) · AMS · Elevated lactate (>2) · Tachycardia
DISTRIBUTIVE
Causes: Sepsis · Anaphylaxis · Neurogenic · SIRS
Skin: Warm & flushed (early) → cool (late)
HR: ↑↑  |  BP: ↓  |  CO: ↑ (hyperdynamic)
SVR: ↓↓  |  PCWP: low/normal
JVP: ↓  |  Lactate: ↑↑
Rx: Fluids + Noradrenaline (1st) ± Vasopressin
⚠️ Sepsis: 30ml/kg crystalloid, cultures, abx <1hr
CARDIOGENIC
Causes: AMI · Acute HF · Myocarditis · Arrhythmia · Valve failure
Skin: Cold, clammy, pale
HR: ↑  |  BP: ↓  |  CO: ↓↓
SVR: ↑↑  |  PCWP: ↑↑ (pulm. oedema)
JVP: ↑  |  S3/crackles
Rx: Dobutamine + Noradrenaline · Diuresis · PCI if STEMI
⚠️ Avoid aggressive fluids — may worsen oedema
OBSTRUCTIVE
Causes: Massive PE · Tension PTX · Cardiac Tamponade · Severe AS
Skin: Cool, clammy
HR: ↑↑  |  BP: ↓  |  CO: ↓
SVR: ↑  |  JVP: ↑↑
Tamponade: Beck's triad (↓BP + ↑JVP + muffled heart)
Tension PTX: Absent breath sounds + tracheal deviation
Rx: Remove obstruction (thrombolysis/embolectomy · needle decompression · pericardiocentesis)
HYPOVOLAEMIC
Causes: Haemorrhage · GI loss · Burns · 3rd spacing
Skin: Cold, pale, dry (non-haemorrhagic)
HR: ↑↑  |  BP: ↓  |  CO: ↓
SVR: ↑↑  |  PCWP: ↓↓
JVP: ↓↓  |  Hct: normal initially then ↓
Rx: IV fluids / blood products · STOP source of loss
⚠️ Haemorrhage: 1:1:1 pRBC:FFP:Plt (massive transfusion protocol)

Step 1 — pH: <7.35 = acidaemia | >7.45 = alkalaemia
Step 2 — Primary: PaCO₂↑ = resp. acidosis | PaCO₂↓ = resp. alkalosis | HCO₃↓ = met. acidosis | HCO₃↑ = met. alkalosis

Indications, Contraindications & Key Notes
✅ Indications
• Type 1 DM (absolute — no endogenous insulin)
• Type 2 DM — inadequate oral control, acute illness, surgery
• Hyperglycaemia in ICU / critical illness (CBG >10 mmol/L)
• DKA / HHS — IV Actrapid infusion
• Hyperkalaemia — insulin + dextrose (drives K⁺ intracellular)
• TPN / enteral feeding — blood glucose control
• Gestational DM / Pregnancy (oral agents contraindicated)
• Steroid-induced hyperglycaemia
❌ Contraindications / Cautions
• Hypoglycaemia — never give without checking CBG first
• Hypokalaemia — correct K⁺ first (insulin drives K⁺ further down)
• NPO patient — withhold bolus insulin (keep basal, reduce by 20–50%)
• Renal failure — dose reduction needed (↓ insulin clearance → prolonged action)
• Hepatic failure — unpredictable glucose; monitor closely
• Mixing glargine/detemir with other insulins — NEVER mix
• IM injection — erratic absorption; always S/C
⚠️ ICU-Specific Pearls
• Only Regular (Actrapid) insulin is safe IV — never give analogue IV
• IV infusion: 50 units Actrapid in 50ml NS = 1 unit/ml
• Transition IV → S/C: give first S/C dose 1–2 hr before stopping infusion
• NICE-SUGAR trial: target 7.8–10 mmol/L in ICU (intensive control ↑mortality)
• Stress hyperglycaemia: common in sepsis, post-op, steroids — treat if CBG >10 consistently
• Enteral feeding: give bolus insulin with each feed · Stop if feed interrupted
• Hypokalaemia: always check K⁺ before starting insulin infusion
• Dawn phenomenon: CBG rises 4–8 AM due to cortisol surge — may need dose adjustment
🚨 Hypoglycaemia Management
CBG < 4.0 mmol/L = Hypoglycaemia
• Conscious: 15g fast carbs (glucose tabs / 150ml juice / 3 tsp sugar)
• Unconscious/IV access: 50ml of 50% Dextrose (D50W) IV stat OR Glucagon 1mg IM/S/C
• Recheck CBG in 15 min · Repeat if still <4
• Once CBG >4: give long-acting carbs to prevent recurrence
• Severe/prolonged: 10% Dextrose infusion · Identify cause (missed meal, excess dose, renal failure)
• Document and review insulin regimen

Brain Death Assessment — Clinical Criteria Checklist ▼
⚠️ PREREQUISITES — Must be satisfied before testing
CLINICAL BRAIN STEM REFLEXES — All must be absent
🫁 APNOEA TEST — Confirmatory (perform last)

Baseline ABG: PaCO₂ 35–45, pH normal before disconnecting
Disconnect ventilator → deliver O₂ 6L/min via suction catheter into ETT

🔬 ANCILLARY / CONFIRMATORY TESTS (when clinical testing equivocal or incomplete)
EEG
Electrocerebral silence (isoelectric) at max sensitivity × 30 min
CT Angiography
No intracranial flow (4-vessel study). Most widely available.
Radionuclide Scan
HMPAO SPECT — absent brain perfusion ("hollow skull" sign)
TCD Ultrasound
Absent/reversed diastolic flow in both MCAs
SSEP
Absent bilateral N20 cortical response to median nerve stimulation
BSA (BAEP)
Absent waves III–V bilaterally on brainstem auditory evoked potential
Number of examiners (Bangladesh/regional): 2 independent physicians required; at least one senior neurologist/neurosurgeon or intensivist. Examiners must not be part of transplant team.
Observation period: 6h minimum after hypoxic-ischaemic injury; 12–24h after other causes — before declaring brain death
Spinal reflexes may persist (Lazarus sign, limb withdrawal) — do NOT invalidate BD diagnosis as these are spinal cord reflexes
Paediatric: Different criteria apply (<37 wks GA → not applicable; neonates → 7 days observation; infants → 2 exams 24h apart)
Organ donation: Brain death = legal death in most jurisdictions. Haemodynamic management critical for organ preservation: MAP 60–80, Na 130–150, glucose 5–10 mmol/L, temp 36–37°C, DDAVP for diabetes insipidus
⚠️ Brain death declaration is a clinical diagnosis. Ancillary tests supplement but do not replace bedside assessment. Follow institutional/national protocol.

BLS & ACLS Algorithm (AHA 2025) ▼
BLS — SEQUENCE
1. Check response → Shout for help → Get AED
2. Check pulse ≤10 sec → If absent or unsure: start CPR
3. Compressions: 100–120/min | Depth 5–6cm
   Hard surface | Allow full recoil | Minimize interruptions
4. Ratio: 30:2  |  Untrained → Hands-only CPR
5. AED: Attach → Analyse → Shock if advised → Resume CPR immediately
6. Advanced airway placed: 10 breaths/min async
2025: CPR feedback devices recommended · Passive O₂ during compressions acceptable
ACLS — SHOCKABLE (VF / Pulseless VT)
Shock (120–200J) → 2min CPR → Check rhythm → Shock
3rd cycle: Adrenaline 1mg IV → repeat every 3–5 min
3rd cycle: Amiodarone 300mg IV bolus
5th cycle: Amiodarone 150mg IV (one further dose)
  OR Lidocaine 1–1.5mg/kg if Amiodarone unavailable
ACLS — NON-SHOCKABLE (PEA / Asystole)
CPR → Adrenaline 1mg IV ASAP → repeat every 3–5 min
Search & treat reversible causes:
5H: Hypoxia · Hypovolaemia · H⁺ (acidosis) · Hypo/Hyperkalaemia · Hypothermia
5T: Tension PTX · Tamponade · Toxins · Thrombosis PE · Thrombosis MI

• TTM: 32–36°C × 24hr if comatose post-ROSC
